American League All-Stars @ National League All-Stars, 5:00p

Summary

The All-Star Game rarely doubles as an actual pitching duel, but this one does, pairing two legitimate Cy Young-caliber arms in Philadelphia's own ballpark. Cristopher Sánchez gets the ball for the NL at Citizens Bank Park as the 29-year-old recently earned his second career All-Star selection, and this will mark his first starting assignment in the Midsummer Classic, becoming the first Phillies pitcher to start the All-Star Game since Roy Halladay in 2011. Across the diamond, Dylan Cease starts for the AL after signing a seven-year, $210 million contract with Toronto and posting a 1.73 ERA with 56 strikeouts over six starts since returning from a hamstring strain, and he nearly threw his second career no-hitter Wednesday against San Francisco before it was broken up in the ninth inning. Both pNERD scores sit comfortably above 9.9, built on absurd xFIP- marks and elite swinging-strike rates, making this a rare exhibition where the stuff matches the stage. Team NERD is understandably a wash here — it's an All-Star roster, not a real team — so this one's entirely about watching two of baseball's best arms attack lineups stacked with the game's best hitters, exhibition stakes or not.
