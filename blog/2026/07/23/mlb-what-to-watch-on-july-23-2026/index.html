Summary

Williams owns a 2.39 xFIP over the last 30 days, which does not align with his 4.70 ERA, and this looks like a good matchup for the numbers to finally catch up. That underperformance shows up in his positive luck component, suggesting better results than his ERA lately would indicate. Taj Bradley isn't chopped liver either, with a 6.87 pNERD boosted by mid-90s-plus velocity, though Bradley holds a clear edge against this specific opponent, owning a 1.54 ERA in four career starts versus the Guardians, including a 10-strikeout outing over seven innings on July 7. On the team side, Cleveland's 7.73 tNERD (aided by a young, inexpensive roster and a strong bullpen) dwarfs Minnesota's underwhelming 2.37, and Cleveland has outscored Minnesota 28-12 across the first three games of this series. With a gNERD of 14.03 - well above both the historical and today's averages - this is one of the more watchable matchups on the board, thanks almost entirely to the pitching.
